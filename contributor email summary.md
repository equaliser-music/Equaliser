## High-level vision

- Build an indie-focused streaming platform that is fair to artists and fans, transparent, anti‑corporate/DIY, community‑based, and not‑for‑profit. [page:1]  
- Operate as an alternative to Spotify’s corporate, opaque, and playlist‑driven model while still being as easy to use as mainstream services. [page:1]  

## Strategic positioning

- Acknowledge strong network effects in streaming; most listeners go where catalog and audience are largest. [page:1]  
- Use “multi‑homing”: coexist with Spotify and other platforms rather than trying to fully replace them or demand exclusivity. [page:1]  
- Avoid full exclusivity deals; instead, rely on occasional exclusives such as first‑listen tracks or unique streams. [page:1]  

## Differentiation features

- Rich band and label pages that effectively act as up‑to‑date websites, with photos, videos, press packs, and other assets. [page:1]  
- Music‑related chat/community features on band pages and possibly broader discussion boards, encouraging interaction between artists and fans. [page:1]  
- Occasional exclusive content: “first chance to hear this track” or “only place to stream that track.” [page:1]  
- Listening parties similar to (but better than) Bandcamp’s implementation. [page:1]  
- Optional listener member pages showing their playlists and enabling social features. [page:1]  

## Values and principles

- Fair royalty distribution that does not heavily skew toward the most‑streamed artists, so that smaller indie acts are properly valued. [page:1]  
- High transparency around royalty deals, playlisting logic, promotion, and any potential conflicts (e.g. “ghost artists”). [page:1]  
- Explicitly anti‑corporate, DIY ethos that aligns with the culture of the indie community and restores a sense of belonging. [page:1]  
- Reduce pressure on artists to create endless non‑music “content” by making the songs and albums central again. [page:1]  

## Community and governance

- Community‑based project that grows a network of musicians and fans who want to co‑create a solution, rather than a top‑down corporate product. [page:1]  
- Non‑profit orientation, with open questions about how to fund startup and ongoing operations while staying aligned with values. [page:1]  
- Potential involvement of activists, authors, and thinkers (e.g. those writing on digital platforms and cultural economics) as advisors or allies. [page:1]  

## Technical and product considerations

- Desire for a decentralised design so the platform can run without a single central controlling middleman. [page:1]  
- Open questions the tech needs to solve:  
  - Where and how to host content, and at what cost. [page:1]  
  - How to manage payments and integrate royalties with PRS/MCPS and similar bodies. [page:1]  
  - How to protect content and prevent bad actors from gaming the system. [page:1]  
  - How to achieve an experience that rivals or improves on Spotify for both artists and fans. [page:1]  
  - How to scale from hundreds to thousands or millions of users. [page:1]  
- Likely use of multiple open‑source P2P/decentralised technologies to let artists control their content and reach fans directly, potentially allowing groups of artists/labels to share infrastructure costs. [page:1]  

## Next‑step process

- Run a UX‑focused exercise with personas (Artist, Label Owner, Fan, Playlist Curator) to define needs and flows before building. [page:1]  
- Start small with a prototype, iterate, and then bring in communities for beta testing and feedback. [page:1]
